📋 Understanding Procedure Complexity Classifications

🔵 Basic Procedures

Simple, straightforward cleft repairs requiring less surgical time and expertise:

  • Straight Line Repair - Direct closure of cleft lip
  • Langenbeck variant - Basic palatal closure technique

🟠 Intermediate Procedures

Moderate complexity repairs requiring more surgical planning:

  • Triangular Variant - Triangular flap technique for lip repair
  • Millard Type variant (Forked flap) - Rotation-advancement technique
  • Rotation/Advancement and Variants - Millard technique variations

🟢 Advanced Procedures

Complex, technically demanding surgeries requiring specialized expertise:

  • Two Flap Palatoplasty and Variants - Comprehensive palate repair
  • Buccal Repair - Using buccal mucosa for reconstruction
  • Push back variant - Posterior palate lengthening technique

🔴 Other Procedures

Procedures not falling into the above categories:

  • Fistula repairs
  • Secondary revisions
  • Other miscellaneous procedures

Note: Fistula repairs are tracked separately in Chart 5

Why this matters: Tracking complexity helps monitor surgical capacity building and program maturity. Higher proportions of advanced procedures indicate growing expertise and ability to handle complex cases locally.
